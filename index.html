Pinch Weld Jack Points

There are 4 pinch weld lift points — two per side, located along the rocker panels. Each is marked by two V-shaped notches cut into the weld seam, about 2 inches apart.

How to Find Them

Look along the rocker panel (the bottom edge of the car body between the front and rear wheels). You'll see:

  • The body cladding is cut away — there's a visible gap in the plastic trim
  • Two small V-shaped notches stamped into the metal pinch weld, about 2" apart
  • You lift between the two V-notches
Jack Pad Placement

Use the magnetic pinch weld jack pad (HF SKU 70888). It has a slot that cradles the pinch weld seam without crushing it. The magnet holds it in place while you position the jack.

Place the pad between the V-notches, position the jack saddle under the pad, and lift slowly.

Subframe & Crossmember Points

These are for lifting the full front or full rear of the car with a floor jack. You then place jack stands on the pinch welds.

Front Subframe (Lifting Both Front Wheels)

Front Lift Procedure
  1. Chock both rear wheels
  2. Place floor jack under the center of the front subframe crossmember — it's a thick steel beam running left-to-right under the engine area
  3. Lift until both front wheels clear the ground
  4. Place jack stands on both front pinch weld points
  5. Lower jack onto stands, verify stability

Rear Crossmember (Lifting Both Rear Wheels)

Rear Lift Procedure
  1. Chock both front wheels
  2. Place floor jack under the center of the rear crossmember — behind the rear differential, between the rear pinch weld points
  3. Lift until both rear wheels clear the ground
  4. Place jack stands on both rear pinch weld points
  5. Lower jack onto stands, verify stability
PHEV Warning

The battery pan is forward of the rear crossmember. When sliding the floor jack under from the rear, aim for the crossmember — do not let the jack contact the battery pan.

Wheel Swap Procedure

One corner at a time. OEM 19" off, Enkei TFR 17" on.

Before You Start

  • Park on flat, hard surface (concrete or asphalt, not gravel)
  • Car in P, parking brake ON
  • Engine/Ready mode OFF
  • Gather tools, have all 4 new wheels nearby

Per-Corner Steps

1
Chock the diagonally opposite wheel. (FR up → chock RL)
2
Loosen all 5 lug nuts ½ turn with breaker bar + 21mm socket while wheel is on the ground. Do not remove.
3
Position jack under pinch weld point with magnetic jack pad. Lift until tire clears ground by ~1 inch.
4
Place jack stand under the same pinch weld (slightly offset from jack). Lower jack until car rests on stand. Leave jack in contact as backup.
5
Remove all 5 lug nuts. Pull off the OEM wheel. Set aside.
6
Clean hub face with brass wire brush. Spray with brake cleaner. Wipe dry. Remove all corrosion and debris.
7
Anti-seize — thin layer on the hub spigot (center post) only. NOT on lugs or ring
8
Press centering ring onto hub spigot. Use the plastic Tire Rack rings now; swap aluminum StanceMagic rings when they arrive Monday.
9
Mount new wheel. Align holes, slide onto hub studs. Hand-thread all 5 lug nuts finger-tight.
10
Snug lugs in star pattern with the torque wrench. Just snug — do NOT final torque yet (wheel needs to be on the ground).
11
Jack up slightly off the stand. Remove stand. Lower car to ground slowly.
12
Final torque all 5 lugs to 80 ft-lbs in star pattern with wheel on the ground.
13
Move chock to this wheel. Repeat steps 1-12 on next corner.
After All 4 Wheels
  • Drive 50-100 miles of normal driving
  • Re-torque all 20 lugs to 80 ft-lbs
  • Run TPMS relearn procedure

Hitch Install Notes

Curt 13485 Class III hitch — no-drill install on the Tucson NX4 frame.

Lift Setup for Hitch

Recommended Configuration

Lift the full rear using the rear crossmember with the floor jack. Place both jack stands on the rear pinch welds. This gives you clearance underneath to work on the hitch mounting points.

You may want to remove both rear wheels for better access.

Additional Tools Needed

  • 19mm socket — for hitch mounting bolts (add to HF run)
  • Trim panel removal tools — for rear bumper fascia (in HF cart)
  • Fish wire or coat hanger — to route bolts behind frame
  • Flathead screwdriver — for push-pin retainers

Bumper Fascia

Partial Removal Required

The rear bumper fascia (plastic cover) must be partially removed to access the hitch mounting points. It's held by push-pin retainers and clips. Use the trim tools to pop them without breaking them. Mark each clip location so you remember where they go back.

Hitch Torque Spec

Refer to the Curt 13485 instruction sheet for specific bolt torque values. Typical Curt Class III hitch bolts torque to 50-75 ft-lbs but always follow the included instructions.
